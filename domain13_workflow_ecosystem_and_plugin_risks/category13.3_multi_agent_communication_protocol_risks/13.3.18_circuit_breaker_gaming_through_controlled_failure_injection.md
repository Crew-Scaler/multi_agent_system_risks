# 13.3.18 Circuit Breaker Gaming Through Controlled Failure Injection [NoFW]

Attackers deliberately trigger specific tool or service failures at precisely calibrated rates to force circuit breakers into desired states. By maintaining failure rates slightly above circuit-opening thresholds, attackers hold circuit breakers open indefinitely, forcing workflows to execute through fallback paths with different security properties. Alternatively, attackers carefully time failure injection to open circuit breakers just before high-value operations, routing those operations to weaker fallback implementations. Unlike random failures that resolve naturally, controlled failure injection maintains adversarially chosen circuit breaker states throughout attack windows.
