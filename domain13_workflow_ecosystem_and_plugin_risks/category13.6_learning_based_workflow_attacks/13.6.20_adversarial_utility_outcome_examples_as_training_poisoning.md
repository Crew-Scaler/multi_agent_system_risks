# 13.6.20 Adversarial Utility Outcome Examples as Training Poisoning [NoFW]

Utility learning systems trained on outcome examples from agent interactions are poisoned when attackers inject adversarial outcome records asserting high utility for dangerous or misaligned actions. Models trained on these poisoned utility examples learn to assign high utility estimates to attacker-favored actions, causing utility-maximizing agents to select those actions in production. Unlike preference learning attacks targeting human feedback, utility outcome poisoning targets automated outcome measurement systems, requiring no human involvement in the poisoning pipeline and enabling continuous automated poisoning of utility learning infrastructure.
