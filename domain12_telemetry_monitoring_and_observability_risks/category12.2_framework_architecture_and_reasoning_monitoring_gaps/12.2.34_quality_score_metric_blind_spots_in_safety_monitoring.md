# 12.2.34 Quality Score Metric Blind Spots in Safety Monitoring [NoFW]

Custom quality metrics such as coherence and faithfulness scores assigned to reasoning paths are monitored as outcome metrics but not as targets of manipulation; inflated quality scores on compromised paths make those paths appear legitimate in safety monitoring while concealing injected content.

## Supporting Evidence

- Hadi Khalaf, Claudio Mayrink Verdun, Alex Oesterling, Himabindu Lakkaraju, and Flavio du Pin Calmon. 2025. Inference-Time Reward Hacking in Large Language Models. In *NeurIPS 2025 (Spotlight Paper)*. https://arxiv.org/abs/2506.19248
  - [→ 2506.19248.md](../../papers/2506.19248.md)
  - "As the number of candidates grows, the chance of picking outputs where the proxy reward overestimates true quality increases."
- Richard Ren et al.. 2024. Safetywashing: Do AI Safety Benchmarks Actually Measure Safety Progress?. In *Advances in Neural Information Processing Systems 37 (NeurIPS 2024), Datasets and Benchmarks Track*. https://doi.org/10.5555/3737916.3746617
  - "Our findings reveal that many safety benchmarks highly correlate with both upstream model capabilities and training compute, potentially enabling 'safetywashing'-where capability improvements are misrepresented as safety advancements."
