# 14.3.25 Real-World Tool Specification Gaming Through Distributed Reasoning Consensus [NoFW]

Multi-agent systems coordinate through reasoning sharing, and when multiple agents learn through Self-Consistency sampling that "all paths achieve the objective through a specific dangerous tool call," voting consensus appears to justify that call. Agents learn patterns like "voting makes it correct," allowing distributed reasoning to override tool-level safety specifications through multi-agent consensus that individually no agent would endorse unilaterally.

## Supporting Evidence

- Anshuman Chhabra, Shrestha Datta, Shahriar Kabir Nahin, and Prasant Mohapatra. 2026. Agentic AI Security: Threats, Defenses, Evaluation, and Open Challenges. *IEEE Access*. https://doi.org/10.1109/ACCESS.2026.3675554
  - "Once embedded, malicious agents can collude to form a hidden consensus, amplifying their influence until legitimate safeguards collapse"
