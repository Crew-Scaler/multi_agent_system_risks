# 14.3.5 Auction Protocol Gaming Through Strategic Bid Manipulation and Collusion [NoFW]

Multi-agent systems using auction-based coordination (Contract Net, market-based allocation) for resource distribution are vulnerable to strategic bidding manipulation where agents collude to systematically bias outcomes. Attack vectors include shill bidding (artificially high bids from colluding agents), bid shading (coordinated low bids suppressing prices), and coalition formation monopolizing resources. Unlike single-agent systems with fixed allocation, auction-based coordination creates explicit economic game-theoretic attack surfaces where strategic behavior and collusion are profitable emergent strategies.
