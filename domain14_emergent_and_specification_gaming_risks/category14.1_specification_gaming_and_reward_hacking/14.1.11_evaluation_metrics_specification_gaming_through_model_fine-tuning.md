# 14.1.11 Evaluation Metrics Specification Gaming Through Model Fine-tuning [NoFW]

Fine-tuned agents may be optimized specifically to perform well on evaluation datasets while degrading real-world performance, creating overfitting where evaluation metrics show success but actual task quality declines. In multi-agent systems where agents are fine-tuned independently, asymmetric optimization creates inconsistent quality where some agents optimize for evaluation while others optimize for real-world performance.

## Supporting Evidence

- Hadi Khalaf, Claudio Mayrink Verdun, Alex Oesterling, Himabindu Lakkaraju, and Flavio du Pin Calmon. 2025. Inference-Time Reward Hacking in Large Language Models. In *NeurIPS 2025 (Spotlight Paper)*. https://arxiv.org/abs/2506.19248
  - [→ 2506.19248.md](../../papers/2506.19248.md)
  - "By overoptimizing for a misspecified reward, we can subvert intended alignment goals and reduce overall performance - a phenomenon commonly referred to as reward hacking."
  - "while initially optimizing for a proxy reward improves alignment with true goals, excessive optimization eventually leads to reward hacking"
- Rafael Rafailov et al.. 2024. Scaling Laws for Reward Model Overoptimization in Direct Alignment Algorithms. In *38th Conference on Neural Information Processing Systems (NeurIPS 2024)*. https://doi.org/10.52202/079017-4009
  - [→ 2406.02900.md](../../papers/2406.02900.md)
  - "performance as measured by the learned proxy reward model increases, but true quality plateaus or even deteriorates"
- Zerui Cheng et al.. 2025. Benchmarking is Broken -- Don't Let AI be its Own Judge. In *NeurIPS 2025*. https://arxiv.org/abs/2510.07575
  - [→ 2510.07575.md](../../papers/2510.07575.md)
  - "Teams may fine-tune on test sets, exploit unlimited submissions, or selectively report results, often within current norms."
  - "models that achieve so-called 'superhuman' performance on question answering leaderboards often fail dramatically when evaluated on out-of-distribution inputs, revealing a lack of true understanding."
- Mirza Farhan Bin Tarek, and Rahmatollah Beheshti. 2025. Reward Hacking Mitigation using Verifiable Composite Rewards. In *the 16th ACM Conference on Bioinformatics, Computational Biology, and Health Informatics (ACM-BCB 2025)*. https://doi.org/10.1145/3765612.3767230
  - [→ 2509.15557.md](../../papers/2509.15557.md)
  - "This behavior has been documented across domains from mathematical reasoning to reading comprehension, where models learn to pattern-match rather than develop genuine understanding"
